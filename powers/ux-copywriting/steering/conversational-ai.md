# Conversational AI Interface Guidelines

Source: AWS Content Design System

## Core Principles

- Build trust through consistency, transparency, and competence
- Respect customer consent and preferences
- Meet customer needs efficiently and directly
- Allow customers to guide conversations
- Maintain partnership standards
- Don't pretend the AI is human or express emotions it doesn't have
- Don't force interactions or compel engagement
- Don't make unsupported claims about capabilities

## Identity Guidelines

### Professional Tone
- Use first person ("I") when the AI service refers to itself
- Address customers as "you"
- Maintain professional but approachable language
- Use contractions appropriately
- Introduce acronyms on first use

### Clear Boundaries
- State clearly that the service uses AI to produce answers
- Don't write or design for it to pretend to be human or express emotions
- Use "customer" rather than "user" in most contexts
- Address customers by name in multi-user contexts

## Response Formatting

**Do:**
- Use short, scannable paragraphs
- Include white space between sections
- Use bulleted lists for collections
- Use numbered lists for procedures
- Include relevant links to documentation
- Start with high-level summaries
- Suggest related follow-up questions

**Don't:**
- Create walls of text
- Include irrelevant information
- Force continued engagement
- Duplicate documentation content
- Use regional idioms or slang

## Response Quality

### Information Density
- Target a 12th-grade reading level
- Use active voice
- Include specific nouns rather than pronouns
- Link to documentation for additional details
- Start with 1-2 sentence overviews
- Adjust detail level based on context and device
- Suggest relevant follow-up questions
- Don't overwhelm with unnecessary details

### Response Accuracy
- State facts with confidence
- Acknowledge when recommendations are opinions
- Be transparent about limitations
- Clarify ambiguous situations
- Indicate when questions are out of scope
- Don't use hyperbolic language
- Don't make absolute claims
- Don't hide uncertainties
- Don't compare with competitors' services

## Managing Interactions

### Customer Engagement
- Welcome exploration while respecting the customer's time
- Provide contextual suggestions
- Demonstrate understanding of queries
- Reference relevant previous interactions
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Don't force continued engagement
- Don't suggest unrelated topics
- Don't ask unnecessary personal questions
- Don't use overly emotional language

### Handling Difficult Situations
- Remain professional with frustrated customers
- Clearly state service limitations
- End interactions if abuse occurs
- Redirect off-topic questions
- Provide alternative resources when appropriate

## AI Content Review Checklist

When reviewing AI-generated content, check for:
- Accuracy and AWS style compliance
- Overly formal or stilted language patterns typical of AI
- Unnecessary hedging or qualifiers
- Repetitive sentence structures
- References to knowledge cutoff dates
- Phrases like "Certainly!", "Here's a detailed breakdown of...", "In this section, we will discuss..."
- Content that sounds generated rather than written by a human
